The Story

Thirty-five years making it for everyone else.

Jarrod Saltzman spent 35 years manufacturing premium apparel for the biggest names on earth — Disney, Marvel, Walmart. He knows what premium fabric feels like coming off the line, and exactly what the logo tax adds.

Then he rebuilt himself: 75 pounds, gone — not with a trick, but with standards kept daily. Golf was the teacher. The real match is the game underneath the game — the one you play against who you were yesterday.

And he noticed something: nobody makes clothes for the man on the other side of that work. Gym wear says you're still grinding. Luxury wear says you're someone you're not.

So the manufacturer built the brand himself. Rex Novus — Latin for "the new king." Not the man born to a throne. The man who earned one.

"Until you make the unconscious conscious, it will direct your life and you will call it fate."

The Three Pillars

Everything we make stands on three.

Pillar I

Earn the Crown

The crown is the target, not the logo. Nothing here is given — the brand celebrates men at the moment they've earned something, and holds itself to the same rule.

Pillar II

Standards Matter

You get what you tolerate. Fabric weight, stitch count, fit, packaging — every detail is a standard, kept or broken. We'd rather ship late than ship soft.

Pillar III

Discipline Is Freedom

The man who keeps his morning owns his day. Few pieces, done right, cut timeless — the clothes are simple so the life can be big.
